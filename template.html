Who We Are
About the Emberbots

We are the Emberbots, a FIRST Tech Challenge (FTC) robotics team from Fremont High School. Since our founding in 2019, our mission has been to spark curiosity and passion for STEM in young people through competitive robotics, hands-on learning, and community engagement.

Our team is made up of dedicated student engineers, programmers, and strategists who collaborate across disciplines to design, build, and compete with our robot each season. We believe that every student has the potential to be an innovator.

The 2024–2025 FTC season, Into the Deep, challenged teams to build robots capable of complex autonomous and driver-controlled tasks — and we rose to that challenge with dedication and creativity.

Engineering
Our Robot

Our robot is a fast, dynamic, and maneuverable bot designed for the Into the Deep challenge. Engineered with precision and tested through countless iterations, it reflects the hard work of our entire team.

Our code was built using block coding and software developed by our hard-working software team. With multiple iterations and testing, we achieved successful autonomous and strong tele-op, usable for different positions on the field.

Viper Slide — high-reach extension mechanism

Rotating Wrist — precise game piece manipulation

Intake + Outtake — efficient specimen collection

Custom Drive Base — agile and maneuverable

CAD-Designed — every component modeled before build

Autonomous + Tele-Op — multi-position capability

Community Impact
Outreach Programs

We believe robotics education should reach every student. Our team runs multiple programs to bring STEAM to our community.

🔦

Torchbearing Tutors

A division of FHS Robotics dedicated to teaching young children about STEAM through hands-on activities and mentorship sessions.

🧪

Kindling Kits

In-home lab kits and experiments provided to students, bringing science and engineering discovery directly into their homes.

🤖

FLL Demo Events

We showcased our robot at a local FLL tournament — 250+ attendees, robot drive time, and nearly 80% of students eager to pursue FTC.

🚁

Matternet Workshop

Our team attended a private event hosted by Matternet, an autonomous drone delivery company. We learned how real-world innovation is applied to engineering and brought those lessons back into our own design process.
